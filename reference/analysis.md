## Design Patterns

### Platform and Template

The site runs on PatientPop, a turnkey dental practice website platform. Styling is controlled by external PatientPop CDN stylesheets which were not directly inspectable. All design values are inferred from structural analysis and PatientPop's standard dental templates.

### Hero Style

- Full-width banner with rotating carousel (PatientPop standard)
- Practice name as primary H1 (locally-optimized, not a brand headline)
- Promotional announcement strip for CTAs
- Phone number is the primary conversion action - no inline lead form in hero

### Navigation Structure

- Type: Sticky horizontal nav bar across full width
- Depth: Two levels (top nav plus dropdowns)
- Phone in nav: Yes, clickable tel: link
- Total top-level items: 9
- Dropdown groups: About Us (2 items), Services (8 items), Patient Resources (4 items)

### Card / Grid Layouts

- Services section: 8 cards in a responsive grid (4 across on desktop, 2 on tablet, 1 on mobile)
- Blog section: 3-up horizontal cards with thumbnail and title
- Provider listing: filterable grid with specialty and acceptance status filters
- Before and After: image comparison gallery (zirconia crowns, porcelain + whitening cases)
- Office tour: 8-image photo gallery

### CTA Patterns

- Primary CTA everywhere: Phone call (323) 887-0088 - repeated in nav, hero, service pages, footer
- Secondary CTA: Online booking via external Modento portal (appointments page only)
- Tertiary: "Call today" inline text CTAs in body copy
- No native contact form on any page - all form interactions route to external services (Modento for appointments, PatientConnect365 for patient forms)

### Footer Structure

- Minimal single-band footer
- 4 links: Privacy Policy, Terms and Conditions, Accessibility Notice, Contact Us
- Contact info repeated (phone and address)

## Notable Observations for the Rebuild

1. Gmail contact address: The practice uses ladentaltown13@gmail.com - not a branded domain email. The rebuild should either establish a branded email or ensure contact forms route correctly without exposing this address publicly.

2. PatientPop URL structure is non-standard: Current paths use /contents/ prefixes (e.g., /contents/appointments/, /contents/about/our-office/, /contents/patient-resources/first-visit/). The rebuild should use clean, flat URLs and implement 301 redirects from all old PatientPop paths.

3. Blog URL pattern: Individual blog posts live at /blog/[slug]/ - not at /[slug]/ at root level (those return 404). Redirect mapping must account for this when migrating blog content.

4. Denti-Cal acceptance is a key community differentiator: Accepting Denti-Cal (Medi-Cal dental for children) is significant for the East Los Angeles market and should be prominently featured in the rebuild's insurance/financial section.

5. Bilingual market signal: One testimonial is in Spanish; East Los Angeles is predominantly Spanish-speaking. The current site is English-only. The rebuild should at minimum consider Spanish-language CTAs or a future-ready structure for bilingual support.

6. Multi-specialty group practice: Six providers across general, pediatric, oral surgery, endodontics, and implant specialties. This differentiator deserves prominent placement in the rebuild.

7. Same-day crown and digital scanning is the core technology message: 5 of 14 blog posts and multiple service page references highlight this capability. Should be a featured technology callout in the rebuild's services or homepage.

8. Testimonial recency gap: 13 of 15 testimonials are from 2019. Only 2 are from 2020 or later. The rebuild should integrate live Google review embeds or establish a regular review update process.

9. No native contact form: All appointment booking routes through external Modento; patient forms route through PatientConnect365. The rebuild should evaluate whether to embed a native form or maintain these integrations.

10. No social media links: No Facebook, Instagram, or other social links appear in the nav or footer. The rebuild could add these if the practice has active profiles.

11. Before and After gallery: Cases include zirconia crown restorations and porcelain crown plus laser whitening combinations. This content type builds trust and should be preserved.

12. Review volume is modest: 4.93/5 from only 15 reviews. The rebuild should make it easy for patients to leave Google reviews to build social proof volume over time.
